# Credit Score Factors: Understanding and Improving Your FICO Score

## Score Ranges and What They Mean
| Score Range | Category | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 800–850 | Exceptional | Best rates on all loan types |
| 740–799 | Very Good | Near-best rates available |
| 670–739 | Good | Most loans approved, decent rates |
| 580–669 | Fair | Higher rates, some denials |
| 300–579 | Poor | Limited approvals, highest rates or secured cards only |

## The Five FICO Factors (with Weights)

### 1. Payment History — 35%
The single most important factor. Every on-time payment strengthens it. Every late payment damages it.

- **30+ days late:** Reported to bureaus, drops score 60–110 points
- **90+ days late (serious delinquency):** Larger drop, stays on report 7 years
- **Collections, bankruptcies:** Severe damage, 7–10 year impact

**Action:** Set up autopay for at least minimum payments on every account. Never miss a due date.

### 2. Credit Utilization — 30%
The ratio of your current credit card balance to your total credit limit.

- **Formula:** (Total balances ÷ Total limits) × 100
- **Target:** Under 30% to avoid impact; under 10% for excellent scores
- **Example:** $1,500 balance on a $5,000 limit = 30% utilization

**Actions:**
- Pay balances before statement closing date (not just due date)
- Request credit limit increases (improves ratio if balance stays flat)
- Never close old cards (reduces total available credit, increases utilization ratio)

### 3. Length of Credit History — 15%
Average age of all accounts. Older accounts signal reliability.

- **Keep old cards open** even if not used — closing them lowers average age
- Minimum recommended history: 3+ years for "Good" range impact

### 4. Credit Mix — 10%
Having a mix of credit types demonstrates you can manage different obligations.

- Ideal mix: revolving credit (credit cards) + installment loans (auto, student, mortgage)
- You do not need every type — do not open loans just for mix

### 5. New Credit / Hard Inquiries — 10%
Each credit application triggers a hard inquiry, which can lower score 5–10 points temporarily.

- Multiple mortgage/auto loan inquiries within 14–45 days count as one (rate shopping protection)
- Hard inquiries stay on report for 2 years but only affect score for 12 months
- Soft inquiries (checking your own score, pre-approval checks) do NOT affect score

## Fastest Ways to Improve Your Score

### Immediate Impact
1. Pay down credit card balances to under 10% utilization
2. Set up autopay on all accounts
3. Dispute any errors on your credit report (free at AnnualCreditReport.com)

### 3-Month Actions
4. Become an authorized user on someone else's old, low-utilization account
5. If starting from scratch: open a secured credit card, use it monthly, pay in full

### 6-12 Month Actions
6. Keep all accounts current and balances low consistently
7. Avoid new applications unless necessary

## Monitoring Your Credit
- **Free credit reports:** AnnualCreditReport.com — all three bureaus annually (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- **Free score monitoring:** Most credit cards and apps (CreditKarma, Experian app) provide free scores
- **Freeze your credit:** When not actively applying for credit, freeze all three bureaus to prevent fraud (free, temporary lift when needed)
