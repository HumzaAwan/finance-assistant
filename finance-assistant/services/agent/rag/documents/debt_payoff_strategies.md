# Debt Payoff Strategies: Snowball vs Avalanche

## The Two Core Methods

### Debt Snowball (Smallest Balance First)
Pay minimum payments on all debts. Direct every extra dollar to the debt with the **smallest outstanding balance**, regardless of interest rate. When it is paid off, roll its full payment to the next smallest.

**Pros:** Fast early wins build motivation. Psychological momentum reduces dropout rate. Works best if you have struggled with discipline in the past.

**Cons:** Pays more total interest over time if small debts have low rates and large debts have high rates.

### Debt Avalanche (Highest Interest Rate First)
Pay minimum payments on all debts. Direct every extra dollar to the debt with the **highest interest rate**. When paid off, roll to the next highest rate.

**Pros:** Mathematically optimal — minimizes total interest paid. Faster total payoff time in most scenarios.

**Cons:** If the highest-rate debt is large, months may pass without a visible payoff. Requires stronger discipline.

## Side-by-Side Comparison

**Scenario:** Three debts, $200/month extra to pay off

| Debt | Balance | Rate | Min Payment |
|------|---------|------|-------------|
| Credit Card A | $800 | 24% | $25 |
| Personal Loan | $3,000 | 12% | $80 |
| Auto Loan | $5,500 | 6% | $120 |

| Method | Order | Total Interest Paid | Payoff Time |
|--------|-------|--------------------|----|
| Snowball | Card A → Loan → Auto | ~$1,850 | ~28 months |
| Avalanche | Card A → Loan → Auto | ~$1,550 | ~26 months |

*(In this case both methods happen to attack the same debt first; the gap widens when smallest balance ≠ highest rate.)*

## Which Should You Choose?

| If you... | Choose |
|---|---|
| Have struggled to stay motivated with debt before | Snowball |
| Are disciplined and math-oriented | Avalanche |
| Have many small debts cluttering your budget | Snowball |
| Have one very high-rate debt (e.g. 29% credit card) | Avalanche |
| Want to free up cash flow quickly | Snowball |

## Hybrid Approach
Pay off any debt under $500 immediately (snowball for speed), then switch to avalanche for the rest. Clears mental clutter fast, then optimizes mathematically.

## What to Do Before Aggressively Paying Debt

### Non-Negotiables First
1. Pay every minimum payment (missed payments damage credit score and trigger penalty rates)
2. Capture full employer 401k match before extra debt payments
3. Have at least $1,000 emergency fund before accelerating payoff

### Debts That May NOT Need Early Payoff
- Mortgage below 4% (investment returns often beat this rate)
- Student loans below 5% with income-based repayment options
- Auto loans below 4%

Debts above 7–8% interest should almost always be paid aggressively before investing.

## Accelerating Payoff

### Finding Extra Money
- Sell unused items
- Take on freelance work for a dedicated payoff period
- Apply tax refunds, bonuses, and windfalls directly to debt before they enter spending accounts
- Negotiate interest rate reduction: call the lender, cite payment history, and ask

### Balance Transfers
Move high-rate credit card debt to a 0% promotional APR card. Pay aggressively during the promotional period (typically 12–21 months). Watch for: transfer fees (usually 3–5%), revert rates after promo ends.
