# Emergency Fund: Sizing and Building Your Safety Net

## What Is an Emergency Fund
An emergency fund is liquid cash reserved exclusively for unexpected expenses or income loss — job loss, medical bills, car repairs, or appliance failures. It must be instantly accessible, meaning a federally insured savings account, not stocks or retirement funds.

## How Much Do You Need

### The 3-to-6-Month Rule
- **Minimum (1 month):** First milestone; better than nothing while paying off high-interest debt.
- **Standard (3 months):** Suitable for dual-income households with stable employment.
- **Recommended (6 months):** Single-income households, freelancers, variable income earners.
- **Extended (9-12 months):** Commission-only, self-employed, or industries with high layoff risk.

### How to Calculate Your Monthly Target
1. List essential monthly expenses only: rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, insurance premiums, minimum loan payments, childcare.
2. Exclude discretionary spending (dining, entertainment, subscriptions).
3. Multiply by your chosen month count.

**Example:** Rent $1,200 + Utilities $150 + Groceries $400 + Insurance $200 + Minimums $100 = **$2,050/month × 4 = $8,200 target**

## Where to Keep It
| Account Type | Typical APY (2025) | Accessibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| High-Yield Savings (HYSA) | 4.0–5.0% | 1–2 business days | Best balance of return and access |
| Money Market Account | 4.0–4.8% | Same day in-branch | May require $2,500+ minimum |
| Regular savings | 0.01–0.5% | Immediate | Too low — avoid for emergency fund |
| CDs | 4.5–5.2% | Locked for term | Early withdrawal penalty defeats the purpose |

## Building the Fund: Tiered Approach

### Tier 1 — Starter ($1,000)
Automate a one-time sprint: redirect all discretionary spending for 4–8 weeks. This covers most car repairs and medical copays immediately.

### Tier 2 — One Month of Essentials
Set up automatic transfer of 10–20% of take-home pay on payday. Reaches one month in 5–10 pay cycles.

### Tier 3 — Three Months
Reduce automatic contribution to 5–10%, sustain until target is met.

### Tier 4 — Full Goal (6 months)
Maintain and replenish after each draw. Stop contributing once full; redirect surplus to investments.

## After Drawing Down the Fund
Replenish before resuming any discretionary savings goals. Treat it as a debt — schedule automatic transfers at the same rate used to build it originally.

## Common Mistakes
- Keeping it in a checking account (too easy to spend accidentally)
- Investing the emergency fund (market drops happen when layoffs happen)
- Including liquid investments as "emergency" money (selling under pressure triggers tax events)
- Counting a credit card as an emergency fund (interest compounds the crisis)
