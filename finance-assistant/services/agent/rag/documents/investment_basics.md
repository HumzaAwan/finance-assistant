# Investment Basics: Growing Wealth Over Time

## Compound Interest: The Core Mechanic

### The Formula
**FV = PV × (1 + r)^n**
- FV = Future Value
- PV = Present Value (starting amount)
- r = Annual rate of return (decimal)
- n = Number of years

### Real Examples
| Starting Amount | Monthly Addition | Annual Return | After 10 Years | After 30 Years |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $5,000 | $200/month | 7% | $39,000 | $236,000 |
| $0 | $300/month | 7% | $52,000 | $340,000 |
| $10,000 | $500/month | 7% | $105,000 | $640,000 |

**Key insight:** Time matters more than starting amount. Starting 10 years earlier roughly doubles the end result at 7% average returns.

## Index Funds: The Foundation of Passive Investing

### What They Are
An index fund holds a basket of stocks that replicates a market index (S&P 500, Total Market, etc.). When the index rises, the fund rises proportionally.

### Why They Beat Most Active Managers
- Low expense ratios: 0.03%–0.20% vs 0.5%–1.5% for actively managed funds
- Broad diversification by default
- No manager risk (no individual making bets)
- Historical data: ~90% of active funds underperform their benchmark index over 15+ years

### Core Fund Types
| Fund | What It Tracks | Expense Ratio Examples |
|---|---|---|
| S&P 500 Index | 500 largest US companies | VFIAX 0.04%, FXAIX 0.015% |
| Total US Market | All US public companies | VTSAX 0.04%, FSKAX 0.015% |
| Total International | Non-US developed + emerging | VTIAX 0.12% |
| Total Bond Market | US government + corporate bonds | VBTLX 0.05% |

### The Three-Fund Portfolio
A globally diversified, low-cost portfolio:
1. US Total Market index fund (~60%)
2. International stock index fund (~30%)
3. Bond index fund (~10%, increase as you age)

## Risk and Time Horizon

### Asset Allocation by Timeline
| Years to Goal | Suggested Stock/Bond Split |
|---|---|
| 30+ years | 90/10 |
| 20 years | 80/20 |
| 10 years | 70/30 |
| 5 years | 50/50 |
| 1-3 years | 20/80 (or savings account) |

### Rule of 110
A rough guide: subtract your age from 110. The result is your approximate stock percentage.
- Age 30: 80% stocks, 20% bonds
- Age 50: 60% stocks, 40% bonds
- Age 65: 45% stocks, 55% bonds

## Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
Invest a fixed dollar amount on a fixed schedule regardless of market price. Buying more shares when prices are low and fewer when prices are high, automatically averaging your cost over time. Removes the emotional temptation to "time the market."

## What to Avoid
- **Timing the market:** Studies show even professionals cannot consistently time entries and exits. Time in the market beats timing the market.
- **Single stock concentration:** Individual stock can go to zero. An index cannot.
- **Frequent trading:** Each trade generates taxes and fees that compound against you.
- **High-fee funds:** A 1% higher fee over 30 years on $100,000 costs ~$100,000 in lost growth.

## Getting Started
1. Open a tax-advantaged account first (401k for match, then Roth IRA)
2. Choose a low-cost index fund (not actively managed)
3. Automate monthly contributions
4. Do not check the balance daily
5. Rebalance annually (sell what grew, buy what shrank back to target allocation)
