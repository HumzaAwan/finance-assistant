# Spending Benchmarks: How Does Your Spending Compare?

## Average US Household Budget (Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2024)
Based on Consumer Expenditure Survey data for a household earning ~$80,000/year pre-tax.

| Category | Average Monthly | % of After-Tax Income |
|---|---|---|
| Housing (rent/mortgage + utilities) | $2,025 | 33% |
| Transportation (car payment, fuel, insurance) | $950 | 16% |
| Food (groceries + dining out combined) | $740 | 12% |
| Healthcare (insurance + out-of-pocket) | $485 | 8% |
| Personal insurance + pensions | $425 | 7% |
| Entertainment | $310 | 5% |
| Clothing | $145 | 2.5% |
| Other (personal care, education, misc) | $520 | 8.5% |
| **Savings (residual)** | **~$500** | **~8%** |

## Recommended Benchmarks vs Actual Averages

| Category | Recommended Range | US Average | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Housing | 25–30% of take-home | 33% | Americans overspend on housing |
| Transportation | 10–15% | 16% | Car ownership is expensive |
| Food total | 10–15% | 12% | On target; dining out drives the high end |
| Groceries alone | 5–8% | ~7% | Cooking at home keeps this in range |
| Dining out alone | 3–5% | ~5% | Common budget leak |
| Healthcare | 5–10% | 8% | Depends heavily on plan type |
| Entertainment | 2–5% | 5% | Streaming + subscriptions add up |
| Savings | 15–20% | 8% | Most Americans significantly undersave |

## Breaking Down Food Spending
Average US household spends ~$740/month on food total:
- **Groceries:** ~$440/month ($55/person for family of 4 households)
- **Dining out / takeaway:** ~$300/month

**Benchmark:** If dining-out alone exceeds $200/month for a single person or $400/month for a family, it is a common cost-reduction target.

## Transportation Benchmarks
Owning one average new car costs approximately:
- Car payment (5-year loan, $35k car at 6.5%): $680/month
- Insurance: $175/month average
- Fuel: $150/month
- Maintenance: $50/month
- **Total: ~$1,055/month per car**

That is 17% of $6,000/month take-home for one vehicle. Two cars often push transportation to 25-30%, crowding out savings.

**Benchmark:** Transportation above 20% of take-home income is a significant budget pressure point.

## Entertainment and Subscriptions
Average American holds 4.5 paid subscriptions spending ~$92/month on streaming and software. Common subscriptions:

| Service | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Netflix | $17–$23 | $204–$276 |
| Spotify | $11 | $132 |
| Amazon Prime | $15 | $180 |
| Gym membership | $30–$60 | $360–$720 |
| Cloud storage | $3–$10 | $36–$120 |
| News subscription | $10–$20 | $120–$240 |

**Total if all above:** $86–$139/month = $1,032–$1,668/year

## Savings Rate Benchmarks
| Savings Rate | Trajectory |
|---|---|
| Under 5% | Emergency vulnerability; nearly no wealth accumulation |
| 5–10% | Slow progress; below average for retirement security |
| 10–15% | On track for retirement at ~65 with employer match |
| 15–20% | Strong foundation; flexibility in retirement timing |
| 20–30% | Accelerated wealth building; potential early retirement |
| 30–50% | FIRE-path (Financial Independence, Retire Early) trajectory |
