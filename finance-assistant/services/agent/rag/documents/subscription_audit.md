# Subscription Audit: Finding and Eliminating Hidden Spending

## Why Subscriptions Are a Budget Problem
Subscriptions are designed to be forgotten. Small recurring charges ($8–$25/month) rarely trigger attention the way large purchases do, but they compound silently. The average American underestimates their subscription spending by 40%.

## How to Run a Full Subscription Audit

### Step 1 — Find Every Subscription
- Download 3 months of bank and credit card statements
- Search for recurring charges: same merchant, same amount, monthly or annual
- Check: credit cards, debit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Venmo (look for recurring)
- Common places subscriptions hide: PayPal auto-billing, iTunes/App Store, Amazon subscriptions, Google Play

### Step 2 — Categorize Each One
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Entertainment | Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, HBO Max, Apple TV+ |
| Music | Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Premium |
| Gaming | Xbox Game Pass, PlayStation Plus, Nintendo Online |
| News / Reading | NYT, WSJ, Medium, Audible |
| Productivity | Microsoft 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, Dropbox, Notion |
| Health / Fitness | Gym, Peloton, Headspace, Calm |
| Shopping / Convenience | Amazon Prime, Costco, Walmart+, DoorDash DashPass |
| Cloud Storage | iCloud, Google One, Backblaze |

### Step 3 — Score Each Subscription
For each subscription, ask:
1. Did I use it more than twice in the last month?
2. Is it core to my work or daily life?
3. Could I replace it with something free (YouTube, library, etc.)?
4. Would I pay for it consciously if I had to re-subscribe today?

### Step 4 — Act

| Decision | Action |
|---|---|
| Not used in 30 days | Cancel immediately |
| Used but duplicated | Keep the better one, cancel the other |
| Seasonal use | Cancel and re-subscribe when needed |
| Used but expensive | Downgrade to cheaper tier or annual plan |
| Annual unused | Cancel before renewal date; set calendar reminder |

## Common Cancellation Tips

### Gym Memberships
- Must often cancel in person or via certified letter
- Many require 30-day notice — cancel before end of billing cycle
- Check for pause options (cheaper than cancelling + rejoining later)

### Streaming Services
- Rotate rather than subscribe to all simultaneously
- Watch a service for 1 month, binge content, cancel, rotate to next
- Share family plans when allowed by terms

### Software Subscriptions
- Annual plans cost 20–40% less than monthly if you will definitely use it
- Check if your employer, bank, or library provides free access (LinkedIn Learning, Adobe, etc.)

## Setting Up Ongoing Subscription Control
- Create a recurring calendar reminder every 3 months: "Subscription audit"
- Use a dedicated email folder for subscription receipts
- Set annual renewal alerts 2 weeks before the charge date
- Check bank statements for charges from companies you do not recognise — these are often forgotten subscriptions or fraudulent auto-enrollments
