# UK Pension Basics — Workplace, SIPP, and State Pension

**Topics:** uk_pension_basics, investment_basics, tax_saving_accounts, saving_techniques

Pensions are the most tax-efficient way to save for retirement in the UK. Contributions benefit from tax relief, employer contributions (for workplace pensions), and tax-free growth within the pension wrapper.

## Workplace Pension — Auto-Enrolment

Since 2012, UK employers must automatically enrol eligible workers into a workplace pension scheme.

### Eligibility
- Aged 22 to State Pension age.
- Earning more than £10,000/year from a single employer.
- Working in the UK.

### Minimum Contributions (2025/26)

The total minimum contribution is **8% of qualifying earnings**:

| Contributor | Minimum rate |
|---|---|
| Employee | 5% (including tax relief) |
| Employer | 3% |
| **Total** | **8%** |

Qualifying earnings are calculated on a band: currently £6,240–£50,270 per year (not total salary). Many employers contribute more than the minimum; always check your contract.

### Tax Relief on Employee Contributions

- **Basic rate taxpayers (20%):** For every £80 you contribute, HMRC adds £20. Effective cost to you: £80 for £100 in pension.
- **Higher rate taxpayers (40%):** Claim an additional 20% via Self Assessment. Effective cost: £60 for £100 in pension.
- **Additional rate taxpayers (45%):** Claim an additional 25% via Self Assessment. Effective cost: £55 for £100 in pension.

### Salary Sacrifice

Salary sacrifice (also called salary exchange) is an agreement to reduce gross salary in exchange for employer pension contributions of the same amount.

Benefits:
- **Saves employee National Insurance** (currently 8% on earnings £12,570–£50,270, 2% above that).
- **Saves employer National Insurance** (13.8%).
- The pension contribution comes from pre-tax salary, so the full gross amount lands in the pension.
- No income tax relief to reclaim separately — it's automatic.

Sacrifice reduces your gross salary, which can affect maternity/paternity pay, mortgage affordability assessments, and state benefits — weigh these trade-offs.

## Self-Invested Personal Pension (SIPP)

A SIPP is a personal pension that gives you direct control over investment choices. Ideal for self-employed workers, higher earners, or those who want to consolidate old pension pots.

### Key Details

- **Annual allowance:** £60,000 or 100% of UK earnings (whichever is lower) for 2025/26.
- **Money purchase annual allowance (MPAA):** Drops to £10,000 once you start flexibly accessing a defined contribution pension (e.g. drawdown).
- **Carry forward:** Unused annual allowance from the three previous tax years can be carried forward if you were a pension scheme member in those years.
- **Lifetime allowance:** Abolished from April 2024 — no longer a cap on total pension pot size, though lump sum and death benefit limits still apply.
- **25% tax-free lump sum:** On retirement, you can typically take 25% of the pension pot tax-free (up to £268,275 in 2025/26). The rest is drawn as taxable income.
- **SIPP providers:** Vanguard, Hargreaves Lansdown, AJ Bell, Fidelity, and Interactive Investor are popular UK platforms.

### What Can a SIPP Hold?

Funds (OEICs, unit trusts), ETFs, investment trusts, shares, gilts, corporate bonds. Residential property is not permitted directly. Commercial property is allowed in a SIPP.

## State Pension

### Full New State Pension (2025/26)

- **£221.20 per week** (£11,502.40 per year).
- Increased annually by the **triple lock**: the highest of CPI inflation, average earnings growth, or 2.5%.
- Available from State Pension age, currently **66** (rising to 67 between 2026–2028).

### Qualifying National Insurance Years

- You need at least **10 qualifying years** of NI contributions to receive any State Pension.
- You need **35 qualifying years** for the full new State Pension.
- NI years are built through: employment (Class 1), self-employment (Class 4), or NI credits (e.g. Child Benefit, Carer's Allowance, Universal Credit).
- **Check your State Pension forecast** at gov.uk/check-state-pension.
- **Buy missing NI years:** Until 5 April 2025, you could pay voluntary Class 3 contributions to fill gaps back to 2006. After this deadline, the standard 6-year look-back applies.

## Pension Strategy Overview

| Situation | Priority |
|---|---|
| Employer matches contributions above minimum | Max the employer match first |
| Higher rate taxpayer | Pension beats ISA for tax relief |
| Self-employed | SIPP or Stakeholder Pension |
| Already maximising pension allowance | Overflow into Stocks & Shares ISA |
| Near retirement (within 5 years) | De-risk into bonds/cash within pension |
