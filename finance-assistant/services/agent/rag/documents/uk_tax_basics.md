# UK Tax Basics — Income Tax, NI, CGT, and Self-Assessment

**Topics:** uk_tax_basics, financial_literacy, saving_techniques

Understanding UK tax is essential for effective personal financial planning. This document covers the key taxes that affect most UK individuals: income tax, National Insurance, capital gains tax, and self-assessment deadlines.

## Income Tax Bands 2025/26

The UK income tax system uses progressive bands. Tax is applied only to the income within each band — not the entire salary.

| Band | Income Range | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Personal Allowance | Up to £12,570 | 0% |
| Basic rate | £12,571 – £50,270 | 20% |
| Higher rate | £50,271 – £125,140 | 40% |
| Additional rate | Over £125,140 | 45% |

### Personal Allowance Taper

For income above £100,000, the Personal Allowance is reduced by £1 for every £2 of income above that threshold. At £125,140, the Personal Allowance is completely withdrawn, creating an effective 60% marginal rate on income between £100,000 and £125,140.

**Tip:** Pension contributions can bring income below £100,000 and restore the Personal Allowance.

### Scottish Income Tax

Scotland sets its own income tax rates on non-savings, non-dividend income. Rates differ from the rest of the UK; Scottish taxpayers should refer to the Scottish Government's published bands.

## National Insurance (2025/26)

National Insurance (NI) funds the State Pension, NHS, and other state benefits.

### Class 1 — Employee Contributions

| Earnings | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to £12,570/year (Primary Threshold) | 0% |
| £12,571 – £50,270/year | 8% |
| Over £50,270/year | 2% |

NI is calculated on a per-pay-period basis (weekly/monthly), not annually — this can create anomalies for bonuses and variable pay.

### Class 1 — Employer Contributions

Employers pay 13.8% NI on employee earnings above the Secondary Threshold (£9,100/year). Employer NI was increased to 15% from April 2025 with a reduction in the Secondary Threshold to £5,000 — check current HMRC guidance.

### Class 2 and Class 4 — Self-Employed

- **Class 2:** £3.45/week for earnings above the Small Profits Threshold (£12,570). Provides State Pension entitlement.
- **Class 4:** 9% on profits £12,570–£50,270; 2% above that.

## Capital Gains Tax (CGT) 2025/26

CGT is charged on gains when you sell certain assets — shares, investment property, business assets.

### CGT Annual Exempt Amount

The CGT Annual Exempt Amount (AEA) was reduced significantly in recent years:
- **2025/26: £3,000** per person (down from £12,300 in 2022/23).

### CGT Rates (2025/26)

| Asset type | Basic rate taxpayer | Higher/Additional rate |
|---|---|---|
| Shares and investments | 18% | 24% |
| Residential property (not main home) | 18% | 24% |
| Business assets (BADR) | 10% (up to £1m lifetime limit) | — |

Main Residence Relief (MRR/PRR) exempts your primary home from CGT.

**Tip:** Spouses/civil partners can transfer assets between each other without triggering CGT. Each partner has their own AEA, effectively doubling the couple's allowance.

### Bed and ISA / Bed and SIPP

Using your CGT allowance to sell investments outside an ISA and repurchase them inside an ISA (bed and ISA) or SIPP is a legitimate tax planning strategy, known as "crystallising gains". Must be done within the same tax year to use the current year's AEA.

## Self-Assessment

If HMRC cannot collect all your tax through PAYE, you must file a Self Assessment tax return.

### Who Must File?

You must file Self Assessment if you are:
- Self-employed with profits over £1,000.
- A company director.
- Earning over £100,000 (even if employed).
- Receiving income from property (rental).
- Receiving income from investments not covered by your basic CGT allowance.
- Making a SIPP contribution above the basic rate threshold (to claim higher rate relief).
- Receiving child benefit while earning over £60,000 (High Income Child Benefit Charge).

### Key Deadlines (2025/26 Tax Year)

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Register for Self Assessment | 5 October 2026 |
| Paper return | 31 October 2026 |
| Online return | 31 January 2027 |
| Tax payment (balance owed) | 31 January 2027 |
| Payments on Account (1st) | 31 January 2026 |
| Payments on Account (2nd) | 31 July 2026 |

Late filing penalty: £100 immediately, increasing to £10/day after 3 months. Interest is charged on unpaid tax from 31 January.

## Tax-Efficient Salary Planning

Strategies to reduce effective tax rate:

1. **Pension via salary sacrifice:** Reduces gross salary, saves income tax and NI simultaneously.
2. **Maximise ISA allowance:** All returns within ISA are tax-free; no CGT or income tax on dividends.
3. **Utilise spouse's allowances:** If one partner is a basic rate taxpayer, consider transferring income-generating assets.
4. **Marriage allowance:** If one partner earns under £12,570, they can transfer £1,260 of allowance to the other (saving up to £252/year).
5. **Charitable giving (Gift Aid):** Higher rate taxpayers can claim back the additional relief via Self Assessment.
